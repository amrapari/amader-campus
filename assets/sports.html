Hand Ball

Quantum Cosmo School: The Regional Champion in the 45th National Summer Sports Tournament

The Quantas of Quantum Cosmo School won the championship in handball and kabaddi competitions, of the 45th school and madrasa summer sport tournament (regional level). After achieving successes in district and sub-regional levels, previously; this time, in 2016, they won in the regional levels. The handball and kabaddi competitions took place in different venues on 5th September. Since, the Quantum Cosmo School won the regional championship last year (2015), they were directly sent for the finals. And, as a result, the semifinal was held between Sylhet and Comilla. In the semifinals, the Ispahani Cantonment Public School of Comilla defeated the H M P High School and College of Sunamganj, reaching towards the finals, with the scores being 15-05.

Gymnastic

Quanta’s success in Singapore Open Gymnastics

The Quantas of Quantum Cosmo School and College have once again glorified the name of Bangladesh in international arena. 16th Open Gymnastics Championship– 2019 took place in Singapore from June 1 to 3, 2019. Gymnasts from eight countries participated in the event. Six out of nine gymnasts from Bangladeshi team were from Quantum Cosmo School and College. Bangladesh won a total of twenty-five awards in the competition, including thirteen gold, five silver and seven bronze medals. Out of the twenty-five, twenty-one awards were won by our Quantas. Individually Quantas won nine gold, five silver and seven bronze medals.

Table Tennis

Quantas Became Champions in Table Tennis and Athletics Tournament

Quantas from Quantum Cosmo School are always ready to win in every given situation as they are spirited to make the impossible things possible. This time, again, the indomitable Quantas became champions in the sub-regional level of 43rd National School and Madrasa Sports Competition. The participating regions were Chittagong, Rangamati, Khagrachari, Bandarban, Feni, Noakhali and Cox’s Bazar. Md. Mostafizur Rahman and Hamim Shanto- Quantas of class Viii and Rengtom Murong – of class IX became champions in the events held at Saint Placid’s High School Chittagong on 13 January 2014. Mostafizur won in the single event by 3-0 sets and Hamim - Rengtom parnership won in the dual event by 3-1 sets defeating the host School – Saint Placid’s School Chittagong.

Band and Paread

The Quantas Stood First in the Event of National Parade, Again

The Quantas of Cosmo school stood first, once again, in the National Parade of Children’s rally, taking place in the Bangabandhu National Stadium on 26th March. The quantas participated for the second time in the parade organized on the Independence Day. And like the first time, they won the hearts of the audience leaving everyone awestruck including the Prime Minister, by exhibiting their talent on the ground. Children from 52 institutes, within and outside Dhaka, took part in the competition. The total number of Quantas in the parade team was 70, from primary and secondary classes. And the marching band was built 59 quantas. And like the previous time, the marching band from Quantum Cosmo School was the only team to be directed by a private institute, that marched with the team of Bangladesh Ansar and VDP, made up of professional instrumentalists and buglers, directed by government institute. Even though this ornate marching band wasn’t a part of the competition, their 3-minutes performance was well appreciated, and mesmerized all the viewers sitting in the audience.

Archery

Quantas Won Gold in Youth Archery Championship

Al-Amin Hossain, student of Class 9 of Quantum Cosmo School, won gold medal in the single event in Teer 2nd National Youth Archery Championship-2018 that took place at the Shaheed Ahsan Ullah Master Stadium at Tongi, Gazipur on November 19 and 20, 2018. In “Below-18 Cadet” category, Al-Amin defeated Ibrahim Sheikh Rezwan of ‘Archer’s Society’ by 4-6 points. Altogether, Quantum Cosmo School Archer Team stood 3rd in both ‘Below-18’ and ‘Below-20 Group’ categories. Soheb Md. Asaduzzaman, Executive Director of City Group, was the chief guest of the two-day-long championship. He inaugurated the ceremony and Rajib Uddin Ahmed Chapal, General Secretary of Bangladesh Archery Federation (BAF), distributed prizes among winners in the closing ceremony.
