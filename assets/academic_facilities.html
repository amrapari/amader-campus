Academic Facilities

Best school in the country year after year

The school, whose students once had to practice on open streets due to lack of a proper field and then choose the types of sports that require less space, is being selected as the best school for sports in the country for two years in a row. Reaching such height of achievement in only one and half decade proves that once a specific goal is fixed, anything is possible! In National School and Madrasa Sports Competition Summer (2016) and Winter (2017), Quantum Cosmo School became Champion in three events (Handball, Kabadi and Table Tennis) and Runner-up in one event (athletics). As a result, Quantum grabbed the ‘Best School for sports in the country’ award.

100% pass rate in public examinations

The pass rate of Quantum Cosmo School and College in public examinations is 100%. This success is evident in PEC, JSC, SSC and HSC –all of the board exam’s results. The reason behind such accomplishment is keeping academic education at topmost priority. Teaching and other academic activities are run in accordance with national curriculum. Expert and experienced management in concerned field selects other books and study materials appropriate for the class students belong to.

Each and every student has a goal

Mostafa is a student of class 2. He is about 6 or 7 years old. Yet, he is very specific about the goal of his life. He wants to become the ‘Headmaster’ of Dhaka University! Little Mostafa doesn’t know yet that the head of a university is called a ‘Vice Chancellor’, not Headmaster. But, since the chief of his school is called a Headmaster, he thinks Dhaka University also has a Headmaster as it is just another educational institute.

Peaceful coexistence of different ethnicities

Since its beginning with only 7 Murong children in Lama, Bandarban in 2001, Quantum Shishukanon has grown to become a community of 2,524 Quantas of 21 ethnic groups other than Bengalis over the years. Quantum Cosmo School is the only school in Bangladesh with so many ethnic and religious varieties. According to a research by Bangladesh Adivasi Forum, there are around 35 tribal groups in Bangladesh. Quantum accommodates students who belong to more than half of such tribes. This includes all the ethnic groups of Chattagram Hill Tracts such as Marma, Mrong or Murong, Tanchangya, Bom, Khiyang, Khio, Tripura, Lusai, Khumi, Chak, Chakma, Rakhain, Pangkhowa and Borua. Moreover, Saontals came from North Bengal and Garo and Hajang children came from Mymensingh and Netrokona. Telegu and Munda communities joined in 2014, whereas Orao and Kora comminutes joined in 2015.
